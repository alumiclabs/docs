## Context

The 6 research documents in the repo root (Opinionated Brief, Executive Summary, Graph-Authoring PDF, Graph-Driven Narrative, Graph Systems research, Game Engine Architecture research) were written before the spec. Many findings from the 2026-04-20 audits are cross-corpus deviations — the spec made a choice that differs from a research recommendation, silently.

The research is not obsolete context; it is adjacent context. A contributor reading a research PDF for architectural reasoning may land on decisions the spec has since moved off (pnpm+Turborepo, xstate-as-backbone, `alumic` package.json subkey, flat-JSON save format, `@Persistent`/`@SaveState` decorators, `NumRef`, three-tier taxonomies, etc.).

Two options: amend the research in place, or freeze it as a historical decision log with forward-pointers to the current spec.

## Decision

Research documents are **frozen as of 2026-04-20**. Each document gets a one-paragraph header added at the top:

```
## SUPERSEDED — Historical Research

This document is historical. Canonical Alumic design lives in `spec/`.
Where this document and the spec disagree, the spec wins.

See the ADR index for decisions that moved since this document was written.
```

Individual research documents that had tractable-scoped recommendations now superseded (xstate as backbone, `alumic` subkey, `NumRef`, pnpm+Turborepo, etc.) also carry a short "Superseded by" footnote pointing at the relevant ADR(s).

## Consequences

**Enables:** Research stays intact as a decision log. Contributors reading the research know where to look for current guidance. The spec is unambiguously the source of truth.

**Obligates:** Any future research exploration must be treated the same way — write it fresh, decide in an ADR, and (if it supersedes prior research) add the corresponding footnote.

**Forecloses:** In-place edits to research PDFs. Research is archaeology once published; new findings go into new documents or ADRs.
